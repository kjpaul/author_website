## Overview

Volume II works through each proposed space launch technology from first principles. For each system (rockets, mass drivers, skyhooks, launch loops, space fountains, space elevators, and orbital rings), the volume develops the governing physics, estimates the materials and power requirements, and identifies the binding constraints.

The goal is not to advocate for a single design. It is to build the comparison tools a reader needs to judge any proposal fairly.

## What's Inside

A chapter per major system, each with the same structure: the physics, the materials envelope, the power budget, the failure modes, and the realistic throughput.
